Research

Approximate Causal Inference: Bayesian inference models have been showing increasing success in modeling many human mental faculties. An important step for this research program is to better understand how humans systematically deviate from ideal Bayesian observers (who perform exact Bayesian inference -- much of which is computationally intractable). This project will look at how task-uninformative visual cues can help in an auditory discrimination task (2AFC) with the goal of estimating the number of samples people draw in a sampling-based approximate causal inference model.

Complement Type Coercion and Word Embedding Models: there is a collection of so-called coercion verbs that seem to be able to perform type coercion, whereby a sentence like "Marty began a book," is able to be understood as, "Marty began [reading] a book." This effect has been used to explore a variety of topics, including the nature of the mental lexicon, the presence of an enriched semantic composition, and semantic processing (independant of syntactic processing). This project will look at whether recent word embedding models, like BERT, capture information about coercion verbs are processed.
